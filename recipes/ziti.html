Scuderi Kids' Fast Fake-Baked Ziti

Description

This Fake-Baked Ziti recipe only takes half an hour to make. Cheesy and easy!

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely-chopped
  • 1 can (28 oz) whole peeled tomatos, preferably San Marzano
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 1 pound ziti rigate
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • A generous grating of fresh nutmeg
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup shredded Asiago cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded Parmigiano Reggiano cheese
  • 1 cup cubed fresh mozzarella cheese

Steps

  1. Put a large pot of water on to boil. Preheat the broiler.
  2. Heat a medium saucepan over medium heat; add evoo, then garlic. Sauté a minute or two; do not let the garlic brown. Chop whole tomatoes and add them to the pan. Add crushed tomatos and salt and simmer 10 mintes. Add basil and simer over low heat 10 minutes more.
  3. When water boils, add salt and pasta and cook 6 minutes, leaving pasta a little chewy.
  4. While pasta cooks, make the béchamel sauce: Melt butter in a small pot over medium heat. Whisk in flour, then salt, pepper, and nutmeg; cook 1 minute. Stir in milk and bring sauce to a bubble. Cook 5 minutes to reduce.
  5. When pasta is cooked, drain and transfer to a big casserole dish. Pour the tomato and basil sauce ove the pasta and turn to coat. Pour the béchamel over pasta--do not mix. Top with Asiago, Parmigiano, and mozzarella. Place the casserole under hot broiler and melt cheese until brown and bubbly, 3 to 5 minutes. Serve immediately, with crusty bread and green salad on the side.
  6. Note: If San Marzano tomatoes are not available, you might want to add a teaspoon of sugar to your sauce.
